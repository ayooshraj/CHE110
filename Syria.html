SYRIA

2010

Population: 21 million

Syria's population in 2010 was relatively stable, with gradual population growth attributed to natural increase and limited emigration.

2011

Population: 21.2 million

In early 2011, Syria plunged into a civil war, leading to internal conflict, violence, and displacement. Despite the onset of unrest, population figures saw a minimal increase due to natural growth in the early months of the conflict.

2012

Population: 20.7 million

The intensity of the conflict escalated in 2012, resulting in widespread displacement, refugee outflows, and loss of life. The population saw a decline due to increased mortality rates and large-scale displacement.

2013

Population: 18.5 million

The conflict continued to escalate, leading to further displacement and a significant decrease in population as people fled the violence and sought refuge in neighboring countries or internally displaced camps.

2014

Population: 17 million

The conflict persisted with continued violence and displacement, resulting in a further decline in population as more people fled the country or were internally displaced.

2015

Population: 16.1 million

Despite efforts to mitigate the crisis, the conflict continued to escalate, leading to ongoing displacement and a decrease in population as more Syrians sought safety outside the country or in safer areas within Syria.

2016

Population: 15.5 million

The conflict's toll on Syria's population intensified, with continued violence, destruction, and displacement contributing to a further decline in population.

2017

Population: 14.6 million

The conflict's impact persisted, leading to widespread devastation, loss of life, and displacement. Population figures continued to decline as Syrians sought safety and refuge both within and outside the country.

2018

Population: 14 million

Despite some areas witnessing relative stability, the overall situation remained dire, with ongoing conflict, humanitarian crises, and displacement contributing to a continued decrease in population.

2019

Population: 13.2 million

The conflict continued to take a heavy toll on Syria's population, with ongoing violence, displacement, and humanitarian crises leading to further declines in population.

2020

Population: 13 million

The conflict's impact persisted into 2020, exacerbating humanitarian needs and leading to ongoing population decline as Syrians continued to face violence, displacement, and instability.
