Natural Effects


Positive Effects:

Economic growth: A large workforce has contributed to China's economic boom, potentially generating more resources for environmental protection efforts.


Clean energy investments: China is a major investor in clean energy technologies like solar and wind power, which can help reduce reliance on fossil fuels and air pollution.


Circular economy: A focus on a circular economy that emphasizes recycling and resource efficiency can help mitigate resource depletion.

Negative Effects:

Resource depletion: China's vast population places a heavy strain on resources like water, coal, and minerals. This can lead to dependence on imports and environmental damage from resource extraction.


Pollution: Rapid industrialization to meet the demands of a large population has resulted in significant air and water pollution.


Water pollution: Industrial waste and agricultural runoff can severely pollute freshwater resources, impacting public health and ecosystems.


Desertification: Overgrazing and unsustainable land use practices in northern China can contribute to desertification.
