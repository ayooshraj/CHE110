CAMBODIA

1975

Population: 7.3 million

Khmer Rouge regime, led by Pol Pot, came to power in April 1975. The regime initiated radical social and agricultural reforms, forcibly evacuating people from urban areas to rural communes. Mass killings, forced labor, and starvation resulted in a significant decrease in population.

1976

Population: 5.7 million

The Khmer Rouge regime continued its brutal policies, leading to further mass killings, starvation, and deaths due to forced labor and harsh living conditions.

1977

Population: 4.8 million

The Khmer Rouge regime's policies persisted, causing ongoing mass killings, starvation, and deaths due to forced labor and harsh living conditions. Additionally, some Cambodians attempted to flee the country to escape persecution.

1978

Population: 5.0 million

Despite international condemnation and the invasion of Cambodia by Vietnamese forces in late 1978, the Khmer Rouge regime remained in power for most of the year. Population figures remained low due to continued violence, starvation, and forced labor.

1979

Population: 5.3 million

Vietnamese forces overthrew the Khmer Rouge regime in early 1979, ending its rule. Many Cambodians who had fled to neighboring countries during the Khmer Rouge era began to return home. However, ongoing conflict and instability continued to affect the population.

1980

Population: 5.7 million

With the end of the Khmer Rouge regime, the population began to stabilize, and some refugees returned to Cambodia. However, the country still faced significant challenges, including ongoing conflict and rebuilding efforts.

1981

Population: 5.9 million

Cambodia started to recover from the devastation of the Khmer Rouge era, leading to a gradual increase in population. However, the country continued to face economic difficulties and political instability.

1982

Population: 6.1 million

The population continued to grow as Cambodia slowly rebuilt its infrastructure and society after the Khmer Rouge regime. Efforts to improve agricultural productivity and healthcare contributed to the population increase.

1983

Population: 6.5 million

The population of Cambodia continued to grow steadily as the country gradually stabilized and reconstruction efforts progressed. However, the effects of the Khmer Rouge regime and subsequent conflicts still lingered.
